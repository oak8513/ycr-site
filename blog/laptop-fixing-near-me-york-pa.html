Common Laptop Problems We Fix Every Week

We see the same laptop issues over and over at our York shop. Slow performance is the most common complaint—usually caused by a failing hard drive, not enough RAM, or a virus bogging down the system. Replacing a traditional hard drive with a solid-state drive (SSD) makes most laptops feel brand new again.

Cracked or dim screens are another frequent repair. Laptop screens are fragile, and one drop or closed lid with something on the keyboard can crack the display. We keep common replacement screens in stock for popular HP, Dell, and Lenovo models.

Overheating and random shutdowns usually mean the cooling fan is clogged with dust or the thermal paste has dried out. Laptops pull in air from the bottom and sides, and York County's humidity plus pet hair creates a thick layer of dust inside the vents. Cleaning it out and reapplying thermal paste solves most overheating issues.

Virus and malware infections slow laptops to a crawl. Pop-ups, browser redirects, and ransomware warnings are signs your laptop needs professional malware removal. We clean out the infection and show you how to avoid it in the future.

When to Search 'Laptop Fixing Near Me' vs. Trying a DIY Fix

Some laptop problems are easy to fix yourself. If your laptop is running slow, try restarting it and checking Task Manager to see what's using up memory. Close unnecessary programs and browser tabs. Run Windows Update and make sure your antivirus is up to date.

If the battery isn't holding a charge, you can often buy a replacement battery online and swap it yourself—most laptops have a removable battery panel on the bottom. Just make sure you buy the correct model number.

But don't attempt a screen replacement or motherboard repair yourself unless you have experience. Laptop screens have delicate ribbon cables and connectors that are easy to damage. Motherboard work requires soldering skills and specialized equipment. If you've never opened a laptop before, you're more likely to make things worse.

Bring your laptop to a professional if: - It won't turn on at all, even when plugged in - You see a blue screen error that keeps reappearing - The screen is cracked or showing strange lines and colors - It's overheating and shutting down randomly - You spilled liquid on it (time matters—the sooner you bring it in, the better) - You suspect a virus or malware infection but can't remove it yourself - You need data recovered from a failing hard drive
