Common Laptop Problems We See Every Week

Most laptop issues fall into a few categories. Here's what brings York residents through our door:

Won't turn on or boot up. Could be the power adapter, the battery, or internal hardware. We diagnose the actual cause — sometimes it's a quick fix, sometimes the motherboard needs attention.

Cracked or broken screen. This is one of the most common repairs. If the rest of the laptop works fine, we can replace just the screen. You'll have your files and programs exactly as you left them.

Runs slow or freezes constantly. Usually caused by a failing hard drive, not enough RAM, or malware bogging down the system. Upgrading to an SSD or adding RAM can make an old laptop feel brand new. If it's malware, we clean it out completely.

Overheating and shutting down. Dust buildup in the cooling system is the usual culprit. We open it up, clear the vents and fans, and reapply thermal paste when needed.

Virus, malware, or ransomware. Your browser homepage changed, pop-ups everywhere, or you're locked out entirely. We remove malware and get your system back to normal.

Physical damage from drops or spills. Hinges break, keyboards stop working, ports get bent. Some repairs are straightforward; others depend on parts availability and extent of damage. We'll give you an honest assessment.

Repair Laptop Near Me vs. DIY: When to Bring It In

Some laptop issues you can handle yourself. Others need professional tools and experience. Here's a realistic breakdown:

You can probably DIY: External cleaning, running Windows updates, uninstalling programs you don't need, or buying a new power adapter if yours is frayed. These don't require opening the laptop.

Bring it in for repair: Screen replacement, motherboard issues, anything involving disassembly, deep malware infections, data recovery from a failing drive, or physical damage. Laptop internals are delicate — one stripped screw or disconnected ribbon cable can turn a fixable problem into a bigger one.

If you've already tried basic troubleshooting and the problem persists, it's time to let a technician take a look. You can find more details about everything we handle on our services page.
