A broken laptop screen looks worse than it usually is. The glass spiderwebs, the colors go weird, half the panel turns black — and most people assume the laptop is finished. It almost never is. The display is its own bolted-in component, and on the vast majority of Windows laptops we replace it without touching anything else inside. York Computer Repair is the walk-in shop for laptop screen replacement in York, PA, and we keep the job simple: bring the laptop to 2069 Carlisle Rd, York, PA 17408, we identify the exact panel your model uses, we order or pull it from stock, and we install it. Most jobs are finished within a few business days, and many common HP, Dell, Lenovo, ASUS, and Acer panels we can handle same-day or next-day if the part is on the shelf.

Not every screen problem is the panel itself though, and that's where bringing it in beats guessing online. A black screen with a working keyboard backlight is sometimes a failed display panel, but it can also be a loose flex cable, a dead backlight inverter on older models, or a graphics-card fault that no replacement panel will solve. Pink or green tinting, vertical lines, and "ghosting" can come from either side. Part of why our diagnostic exists is to make sure you don't pay for a $300 screen when the real fault is a $5 cable — or, worse, pay for a screen on a laptop whose motherboard is the actual problem.

How a Screen Replacement Works at Our Shop

When you walk in with a broken display, we start by looking up your exact laptop model — make, generation, and panel SKU. Two laptops with the same model number can ship with different screens (matte vs. glossy, touch vs. non-touch, FHD vs. HD), and ordering the wrong panel costs everyone time. We pull the cover off, confirm the part number printed on the back of the existing panel, and check whether the digitizer (the touch glass) is bonded to the LCD or separate. That single check is what determines whether you need a $90 part or a $300 part.

From there we run a quick functional check on the rest of the laptop while it's open — keyboard, trackpad, ports, charging circuit, hinges, fans — so a hidden second problem doesn't surprise you a week after the screen is fixed. The diagnostic fee is a flat $39.99 for standard laptops and $99 for gaming laptops, and that fee applies toward the repair if you choose to move forward. We don't run "free" estimates — that usually means a markup buried somewhere else, and we'd rather just tell you up front what something costs.

Most laptop screen replacements are finished in 1–3 business days from drop-off. Common HP, Dell, and Lenovo consumer panels we often have on the shelf and can complete same-day or next-day. Less common panels — high-resolution gaming laptops, business ultrabooks, and 4K touch displays — usually need to be ordered, which adds a couple of days. We'll always tell you the realistic turnaround on day one before any paid work begins.

Can you replace a touchscreen laptop display? +

Yes. Touchscreens are more involved because the digitizer is often bonded to the LCD as a single assembly, and the part costs more, but we handle them regularly. Bring it in and we'll confirm the correct panel for your exact model.

My screen looks fine but the image is flickering — is that the panel? +

Sometimes — but not always. Flickering can come from the display itself, a loose or damaged display cable inside the hinge, a graphics driver fault, or in rarer cases the GPU. Our diagnostic isolates which one it is so you don't pay for a panel you don't need.
