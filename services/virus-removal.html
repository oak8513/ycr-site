Modern infections are nothing like the old "joke" viruses of fifteen years ago. Today's malware is built to make money — by stealing saved passwords, hijacking your browser to inject ads, encrypting your photos and demanding payment, or quietly mining cryptocurrency in the background while your laptop bakes itself. Many of them survive a "factory reset" if you don't know where to look. That's why a quick scan with whatever free tool came pre-loaded on the machine usually isn't enough. A proper cleanup removes the active threat, finds the persistence mechanism (scheduled task, service, registry run-key, browser extension, rogue update agent), and then verifies the system is actually clean before handing it back.
