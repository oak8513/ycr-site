Desktops fail differently than laptops. They run for years on dust, electricity, and luck, until one day a power supply quietly drops dead, a stick of RAM throws an error, a Windows update corrupts the boot sector, or the GPU starts artifacting. Most of these failures look identical from the outside ("it just won't turn on"), so a real diagnosis matters — guessing parts gets expensive fast. Our technicians have been doing this long enough to recognize the symptom patterns and isolate the actual culprit on the bench, not by trial and error in your living room.

How a Desktop Repair Works at Our Shop

The process is the same whether you're dropping off a family PC or a six-fan gaming rig. You walk in (or call ahead — either is fine), we ask a few questions about what the machine is doing, and we accept the unit. Just bring the tower itself; you don't need to lug the monitor and cables along. Once it's on the bench we run a hands-on diagnostic, then call or message you with the actual problem and what it would cost to fix. No paid work begins without your okay.

Our diagnostic fee is a flat $39.99 for standard desktops and $99 for gaming PCs — the higher rate covers the extra time required to test high-end CPUs, discrete GPUs, custom liquid cooling loops, and overclocked configurations. The diagnostic fee applies toward the repair if you choose to move forward. We don't offer "free diagnostics," because in our experience that phrase usually means costs are hidden somewhere else, and we'd rather just be honest with you about pricing up front.

Most desktop repairs are finished in 1–3 business days. Simple jobs like a fresh Windows install, a virus cleanup, a power supply swap, or a RAM/SSD upgrade are often same-day. If a repair needs a part shipped from out of state — say, a specific GPU or a less common motherboard — you'll know on day one, not a week into waiting.
